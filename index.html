About the Event

The Standards Hackathon is a 48-hour innovation challenge organized by the BIS Standards Club, VIT Vellore in association with the Bureau of Indian Standards, Chennai as part of Gravitas 2025, VIT's annual techno-management fest.

This event brings together creative minds to develop innovative solutions that align with Indian Standards, fostering interdisciplinary teamwork on real-world problems.

Objectives

  • Promote awareness of Indian Standards in engineering and innovation
  • Foster interdisciplinary teamwork on real-world problems
  • Encourage tech-based compliance, safety, and sustainability

In Association With

Innovation Tracks

Each team will work under one of the following BIS-aligned tracks

Smart Infrastructure & Urban Tech

Use BIS civil/structural codes like IS 875 & IS 16700 to build smart, compliant infrastructure tools.

Healthcare & Biomedical Standards

Medical devices, sanitation & hygiene tech using standards like IS 13450, IS 15700.

Environmental Sustainability

Innovations around water, air, waste, and green systems – aligned with IS 10500, IS 5182.

Consumer Safety & Product Quality

Verify labels, test product safety, or create alert systems using standards like IS 15700.

Energy & Electrification

Battery, LED, solar system innovations with standards like IS 16046 and IS 16102.

Digital Systems & Cybersecurity

AI ethics, secure systems & data compliance based on IS 17428 & ISO/IEC standards.

Open Innovation (Wildcard)

For impactful ideas that don't fit a single domain but align with BIS principles.

Mechanical Systems & Manufacturing

Innovations in manufacturing, design, and automation based on IS 210, IS 2062, IS 10218, and other mechanical standards.

Topic Selection Process

Track Selection (Pre-Event)

During registration, each team will select one of the eight BIS-aligned innovation tracks based on their area of interest and expertise.

Problem Statement Allocation

At the start of the event, the organizing team will release 2–3 curated problem statements per track, designed in consultation with BIS Chennai and academic mentors.

Option for Custom Topics

Teams may propose their own problem statements under the selected track, subject to review and approval by faculty mentors.

Mentorship & Guidance

Throughout the hackathon, technical and domain mentors will be available to clarify standards and validate feasibility of ideas.
